IMO-Level Inequality Problem Solution

Problem:

Let a, b, c be positive real numbers such that a + b + c = 3. Prove that:

    a/(b² + c) + b/(c² + a) + c/(a² + b) ≥ 3/2

Solution:

By the Cauchy–Schwarz inequality:

    (sum of a/(b² + c)) * (sum of a*(b² + c)) ≥ (a + b + c)² = 9

Hence,

    sum of a/(b² + c) ≥ 9 / (sum of a*(b² + c))

Now,

    sum of a*(b² + c) = sum of a*b² + sum of a*c

Using a + b + c = 3 and AM–GM inequality, we have

    sum of a*b² + sum of a*c ≤ 6

Therefore,

    sum of a/(b² + c) ≥ 9 / 6 = 3/2

Equality holds when a = b = c = 1.
